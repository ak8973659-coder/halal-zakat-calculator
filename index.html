What is Zakat?

Zakat is the third pillar of Islam. It is an obligatory act of charity requiring Muslims to donate 2.5% of their qualifying wealth each year to help those in need.

Zakat purifies your wealth and is a means of social justice in society, redistributing wealth and helping to alleviate poverty.

Who Should Pay Zakat?

Zakat is obligatory for every Muslim who has reached puberty, is of sound mind, and has wealth above the nisab threshold for one lunar year.

What is Nisab?

Nisab is the minimum amount of wealth a Muslim must possess before they are obliged to pay Zakat. This is typically equivalent to the value of 87.48 grams of gold or 612.36 grams of silver.
